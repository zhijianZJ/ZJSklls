# AI Career Map

Use five evidence dimensions when comparing directions: desired result; current work and project experience; time, budget, equipment, language, and learning conditions; observed response to relevant tasks; and the result of a minimum experience task.

The map has five visible directions. AI Operations is split into two work branches, producing six distinct minimum experience tasks.

## AI Agent Development

- **Work object:** Model-, API-, tool-, and workflow-based systems.
- **Strong evidence:** Independently builds, inspects, debugs, evaluates, and delivers a bounded Agent or automation.
- **Common false positive:** A copied demo runs once.
- **Primary risk:** Weak software fundamentals make failures opaque and delivery unreliable.
- **Minimum experience task:** Complete a bounded model/API task and diagnose one induced failure.

## Vibe Coding / AI Application Building

- **Work object:** User-facing software built with AI assistance.
- **Strong evidence:** Understands, changes, tests, debugs, and deploys assisted code.
- **Common false positive:** Generating code or a polished screen without understanding or testing it.
- **Primary risk:** Fast generation hides correctness, security, and maintenance gaps.
- **Minimum experience task:** Build a small tool, then implement and verify one changed requirement.

## AI Product Management

- **Work object:** User and business problems translated into testable AI product decisions.
- **Strong evidence:** Defines capability boundaries, failure cases, a prototype, metrics, and acceptance criteria.
- **Common false positive:** Producing feature ideas or prompt lists without user evidence or trade-offs.
- **Primary risk:** Treating probabilistic model behavior as a deterministic feature.
- **Minimum experience task:** Specify one AI feature with limitations, failure cases, and acceptance criteria.

## AI Operations: Content/Growth

- **Work object:** Content, audience, user, distribution, and growth loops.
- **Strong evidence:** Produces a measurable operating result and can explain the causal contribution.
- **Common false positive:** High content volume, tool use, or impressions without a validated outcome.
- **Primary risk:** Optimizing output quantity while ignoring audience response and business value.
- **Minimum experience task:** Complete one research-to-distribution loop and review its observed result.

## AI Operations: Business Efficiency

- **Work object:** Enterprise processes, handoffs, controls, and bounded automation opportunities.
- **Strong evidence:** Improves a real process with measured time, quality, cost, or risk results.
- **Common false positive:** Counting automations without validating adoption or process impact.
- **Primary risk:** Automating an unclear or unsafe process and scaling its defects.
- **Minimum experience task:** Map one real process and validate one bounded automation point with its user.

## AI Tools and Workplace Application

- **Work object:** Real role-specific deliverables and workflows improved with AI tools.
- **Strong evidence:** Shows repeatable improvement in quality, time, verification, or collaboration.
- **Common false positive:** Knowing many tools or producing one unverified draft.
- **Primary risk:** Mistaking a cross-role capability for a standalone job market.
- **Minimum experience task:** Redo one real work deliverable and compare before/after quality or time.

AI Tools and Workplace Application is usually a cross-role capability rather than automatically a standalone job.
